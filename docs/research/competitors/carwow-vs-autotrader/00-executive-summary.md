## The One-Line Verdict

> Neither Carwow nor Auto Trader has built for the EV buyer.  
> EVGuide has a 3–5 year window to own this market before incumbents wake up.

---

## 1. Key Insights from All 5 Agents

### Agent 1 — Design
- Carwow has the best consumer design in UK automotive (8.5/10) but treats EVs as just another fuel type
- Auto Trader is functional but dated, orange palette limits premium positioning (6.5/10)
- **Gap:** No platform has built EV-native UI — range maps, charging overlays, battery health indicators — these do not exist

### Agent 2 — User Flows
- Carwow's reverse auction model creates urgency and higher lead quality
- Auto Trader's saved search + price alert system is strong for retention
- **Gap:** Neither platform starts with EV buyer's actual questions (range, charging, cost). Both assume the buyer already knows what they want

### Agent 3 — CTAs & Functions
- Carwow's sticky "Get best price" CTA and simplified lead form are conversion benchmarks
- Auto Trader's "Price drop alert" and ATCheck product are smart retention and revenue tools
- **Gap:** Zero EV-specific CTAs exist anywhere — "Does this fit my commute?", "Check home charger compatibility", "Battery health report" — none of these exist on any competitor

### Agent 4 — Database Architecture
- Both platforms run ICE-optimised data models (DVLA data, MOT history, mileage)
- **Gap:** No competitor captures battery health, real-world range profiles, charging compatibility, TCO modelling, or salary sacrifice calculations — these are both product features and data moats

### Agent 5 — Business Model
- Auto Trader: ~£550M ARR, 65%+ margins, dealer subscription dominance
- Carwow: ~£80–100M ARR, CPL lead gen + manufacturer partnerships
- **Gap:** Neither has a single EV-specific revenue line. Battery reports, salary sacrifice affiliate, home charger install, EV energy tariff switching — zero coverage

## 4. What EVGuide Should Copy

### From Carwow
- **Reverse auction lead model** — don't send leads to one dealer, send to competing dealers. Creates urgency, better prices for buyers, justifies premium CPL from dealers
- **Sticky single-intent CTA** — one primary action per page, always visible on scroll
- **Budget-first finance flow** — enter monthly budget → matching inventory shown
- **Lightweight lead form** — name, email, postcode only. Reduce friction.
- **High-quality, consistent photography standards** — set minimum quality for listings
- **Seller valuation via reg number** — lowest friction entry into sell funnel

### From Auto Trader
- **Price intelligence system** — "Great Price / Fair Price" badge based on market comparison
- **Saved search + price drop alerts** — critical retention mechanism
- **Vehicle history product** — but EVGuide's version should include battery health (not just MOT)
- **Dealer subscription tiers** — predictable recurring revenue, not just CPL
- **Comparison tool** — side-by-side up to 4 vehicles
- **Map view for charging stations** — AT has map-based search, EVGuide should have charging map overlay

## 5. What EVGuide Should Avoid

| Avoid | Reason |
|---|---|
| Orange as primary brand color | Limits premium positioning. Associated with AT's utilitarian feel |
| Filter overload on listing pages | Analysis paralysis. Show 5 key filters, progressive disclosure for the rest |
| Ad/sponsored placement disrupting browse | Destroys trust. Early-stage, prioritise user experience over display revenue |
| Email-only offer delivery (Carwow's weakness) | Keep post-lead experience on-platform. Build account/dashboard |
| Generic ICE-focused CTA language | "Send enquiry" and "Contact dealer" are invisible. Use EV-native copy |
| Starting with private seller listings | Complex trust/fraud problem. Focus on vetted EV dealers first |
| Copying AT's mega-spec pages | Long scrolling spec pages without EV context are overwhelming. Tabbed EV-native layout instead |
| Building for ICE features (MPG, engine size, etc.) | These are irrelevant to EV buyers. Don't inherit ICE mental models |

## 6. Where EVGuide Can Be Genuinely Better

These are areas where EVGuide starts with a **structural advantage** — not just a feature gap to fill, but a fundamentally different and better product:

### 1. Range Confidence Engine
- "Does this EV fit my daily commute?" — enter postcode pair and daily mileage
- Show: range vs commute, surplus range, seasonal variance, real-world vs WLTP
- **Neither competitor has this. It is the #1 EV purchase anxiety.**

### 2. Battery Health as a First-Class Citizen
- Battery health score on every EV listing
- Certified reports available as a product
- Valuation adjusted for battery state
- **This changes EV used car trust from guesswork to verified data**

### 3. True Total Cost of Ownership
- Compare: monthly finance + energy cost + insurance + servicing vs current petrol/diesel car
- Show payback period, 3-year and 5-year TCO
- Adjustable for user's energy tariff, charging mix, annual mileage
- **Neither platform models this. Most buyers don't understand EV economics until EVGuide shows them**

### 4. Salary Sacrifice Marketplace
- Connect buyers with employer SS schemes
- Show net cost after tax saving (often 30–40% cheaper than personal purchase)
- Revenue: affiliate commission from SS providers
- **£3B+ market in UK. Zero automotive platforms serving it well**

### 5. Charging Intelligence
- Filter by: charging standard (CCS/Type2/CHAdeMO), max charge speed
- Show nearby public charging on vehicle detail page
- "Home charging compatible" badge
- Estimated charge cost at current energy prices

### 6. EV Valuation with Battery Adjustment
- Current platforms value EVs like ICE cars (mileage + age only)
- EVGuide adds: battery health %, charging history, warranty remaining
- **More accurate valuations = more dealer trust = premium CPL rates**

## 8. Final Positioning: EVGuide as an EV Intelligence Platform

### The Market Narrative

The UK car market is at an inflection point. ICE vehicle sales will decline every year from now on. By 2030, ICE new car sales are banned. By 2035, near-universal EV adoption is the legal trajectory.

Carwow and Auto Trader were built for the ICE era. They will adapt — but slowly, from a position of defending existing revenue rather than innovating. Their dealer bases are ICE-oriented. Their data models are ICE-shaped. Their UX assumes petrol buyers.

**EVGuide enters at exactly the right moment** — early enough to build before the market tips, late enough to have a clear product direction.

### What EVGuide Is

Not a car listing site that happens to include EVs.  
Not a comparison tool with an electric filter.

**EVGuide is an EV intelligence platform** — the authoritative place for UK buyers to:
- Understand if an EV fits their life
- Find the right EV at the right price with a verified battery
- Know what it really costs, including all the savings they're not seeing elsewhere
- Buy with confidence through a transparent, dealer-competitive process

### Competitive Positioning Statement

> *"Carwow helps you get the best deal on any car.  
> Auto Trader shows you every car for sale.  
> EVGuide helps you find the right electric car for your life — and proves you can afford it."*
