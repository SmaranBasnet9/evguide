## 1. Carwow — Business Model

### Company Overview
- Founded: 2011, London
- Model: Consumer-facing marketplace + dealer network
- Revenue range: ~£80–100M ARR (2023 estimates, private company)
- Key markets: UK, Germany, Spain, France, Italy
- Acquired: Wizzle (car selling platform, 2021)
- Investors: Episode 1, Balderton Capital, Accel (Series D+)

### Revenue Streams

#### 1. Lead Generation (Primary — est. 60–70% of revenue)
- **Mechanism:** Dealers pay per validated lead (CPL model)
- **Lead types:**
  - New car enquiries (manufacturer partner leads) — highest value
  - Used car enquiries — mid value
  - Part-exchange / sell leads — lower value
- **Pricing (inferred):** £20–£150 per qualified lead depending on vehicle type and segment
  - New premium vehicle lead (BMW, Mercedes) → higher end
  - Used sub-£10k vehicle → lower end
- **Volume:** Millions of leads per year across markets
- **Key insight:** Carwow's reverse auction model means they can charge a premium per lead because dealers know they're competing — higher urgency = higher close rate = higher CPL justified

#### 2. Manufacturer Partnerships (est. 20–25% of revenue)
- **Mechanism:** Car manufacturers pay Carwow for featured placement, campaign management, and new car configuration traffic
- **Products:**
  - Featured make/model placement on homepage and listings
  - "Configure & get price" flow for new cars (Carwow as OEM channel)
  - Campaign-specific landing pages
  - Audience targeting using Carwow's first-party buyer intent data
- **Key partners:** BMW, Volkswagen Group, Stellantis, Renault Group
- **Key insight:** This is the highest-margin revenue stream — no per-lead cost, recurring partnership fees + performance bonuses

#### 3. Finance Commission (est. 10–15% of revenue)
- **Mechanism:** When a buyer takes out finance through Carwow-connected lenders, Carwow earns a commission
- **Structure:** % of finance value (typically 1–3%) or flat fee per funded deal
- **FCA implications:** Carwow must be FCA authorised or work through an authorised credit broker
- **Key insight:** Finance penetration in UK car buying is ~85% — this is a large addressable opportunity

#### 4. Insurance Affiliate (est. 2–5% of revenue)
- **Mechanism:** Referral fee when a buyer clicks through to insurance partners
- **Partners:** Confused.com, CompareTheMarket, direct insurers
- **Low margin but zero cost to serve**

#### 5. International Expansion (emerging)
- Same model being rolled out across EU markets
- Revenue from German/Spanish market growing

### Carwow Revenue Model Strengths
- High-intent lead quality → premium CPL pricing
- Manufacturer partnerships = recurring high-margin revenue
- Network effect: more dealers → better prices → more buyers → more leads
- Finance commission scales with car prices (EV uplift natural)

### Carwow Revenue Model Weaknesses
- CPL model creates tension with dealers (quality disputes, churn)
- No platform-side product revenue (no vehicle history check, no data products)
- Revenue per user is low for browsers who don't convert to lead
- Post-lead experience is off-platform → limited retention revenue

## 2. Auto Trader — Business Model

### Company Overview
- Founded: 1977 (print), digital transformation 2013
- Listed: LSE (AUTO) — FTSE 100 constituent
- Revenue: ~£550M (FY2024)
- EBITDA margin: ~65%+ — one of the highest-margin businesses in UK tech
- Market cap: ~£7B (2024)
- Dominant market position: 70%+ of UK online car search traffic

### Revenue Streams

#### 1. Dealer Subscriptions (Primary — ~75% of revenue)
- **Mechanism:** Dealers pay monthly subscription to list inventory on Auto Trader
- **Tier structure (approximate):**
  - Starter: ~£500–800/mo (small dealers, limited stock)
  - Standard: ~£1,000–1,500/mo
  - Advanced: ~£2,000–3,000/mo (enhanced analytics, priority placement)
  - Premium/Manufacturer: £5,000–15,000+/mo (flagship dealer groups, OEMs)
- **~13,000 active dealer accounts** (public data)
- **Key insight:** Subscription model = predictable recurring revenue, very high retention because dealers can't afford not to be on AT (where buyers look first)

#### 2. Consumer Products (est. 8–12% of revenue)
- **ATCheck / Vehicle History:** £9.99–£29.99 per check (full history report)
  - MOT history, mileage records, finance checks, written-off status
  - High margin, low cost to serve (data licensing from DVLA, HPI, etc.)
- **Listing upgrades for private sellers:**
  - Highlighted listing: ~£3–5 extra
  - Featured listing: ~£10–15 extra
  - Top of search: variable auction pricing
- **Valuation reports:** Upsell from free valuation to detailed report

#### 3. Finance (est. 8–10% of revenue)
- **Mechanism:** White-label finance brokerage with multiple FCA-authorised lenders
- **Commission:** % of finance value per funded deal
- **Volume:** Very high due to AT's traffic dominance
- **Key insight:** AT Finance is a major strategic asset — they have buyer intent data AND finance capability, creating an end-to-end sale

#### 4. Insurance (est. 2–3%)
- Affiliate revenue from insurance comparison integration
- Co-branded products with select insurers

#### 5. Data & Analytics Products (est. 3–5%)
- **Dealer Data:** Market pricing intelligence, stock turn analysis, competitive benchmarking
- **Manufacturer Data:** New car pricing strategy data, competitive positioning
- **Retail Finance Data:** Trends and lender performance data
- **Key insight:** AT sits on the most valuable automotive market data in the UK — this is an undermonetised but growing revenue stream

#### 6. Digital Retailing Tools (emerging)
- Online reservation (reserve a car with a deposit)
- Online part-exchange valuation tools sold to dealers
- CRM/DMS integrations for dealer workflow

### Auto Trader Revenue Model Strengths
- Subscription = predictable, high-retention revenue
- Market dominance creates pricing power (dealers have no alternative)
- Consumer products (ATCheck) = high-margin direct revenue
- Data products = growing, high-margin, defensible
- FTSE 100 — access to capital for acquisitions

### Auto Trader Revenue Model Weaknesses
- Dealer subscription model creates adversarial relationship (dealers would leave if they could)
- Consumer experience is optimised for volume, not quality
- No EV-specific revenue streams
- Ad-heavy listings reduce user trust
- Innovation slowing due to dominance — no urgency to differentiate

## 4. EVGuide Monetisation Roadmap

EVGuide is entering as an **EV-specialist platform** in a market where both incumbents treat EVs as a fuel type. This creates the opportunity to:
1. Own EV buyer trust through superior content, tools, and data
2. Capture premium CPL rates for high-value EV buyers
3. Build data products competitors cannot replicate
4. Become the authoritative EV intelligence layer between buyers, dealers, and manufacturers

## Summary

Auto Trader is a £550M revenue, 65%+ margin subscription beast with an unassailable market position in ICE-era automotive. Carwow is a well-funded, high-quality lead gen business that has innovated with its reverse auction model.

**Neither has a EV strategy. Neither is building for the EV transition.**

EVGuide's monetisation opportunity is not to compete with these platforms on their terms — it is to build the **first native EV business model** that captures the transition:

- **Short term:** CPL leads from EV dealers who get better quality than ICE-focused platforms
- **Medium term:** Consumer products (battery reports, TCO calculators) that ICE platforms have no reason to build
- **Long term:** B2B data products that become the market intelligence layer for UK EV pricing, battery health standards, and fleet electrification

The market transition from ICE to EV creates a **structural window** for a new entrant. This window is open for approximately 3–5 years before incumbents respond. EVGuide must move fast.
